# Global Design Preferences and Cultural Habits

## Purpose
Before building a website for any target market, understand regional design preferences, cultural habits, color associations, trust signals, and UX conventions. A site that converts well in one country may fail in another due to cultural mismatches. This reference helps the agent choose appropriate design directions based on the target market.

## Regional Design Profiles

### North America (USA, Canada)
#### Color Preferences
- Trust colors: Blue (#0066CC, #1a73e8), Green (growth, money)
- Action colors: Orange, Red for CTAs and urgency
- Preferred palettes: Clean, high-contrast, corporate blues with white space
- Avoid: Overly dark themes for mainstream ecommerce (except tech/luxury niches)
#### Layout Conventions
- Preference for generous white space
- Hero sections with large product imagery
- Minimal navigation with clear category labels
- Sticky headers with search prominent
- Trust badges near checkout (SSL, payment icons, review stars)
#### Typography
- Sans-serif fonts: Inter, Helvetica, Arial, Roboto
- Large readable text (16px+ base)
- Clear hierarchy with bold headings
#### Trust Signals
- Customer reviews and ratings (critical — display prominently)
- BBB accreditation, Trustpilot
- Free shipping thresholds clearly displayed
- Return policy visible on product pages
- Social proof (customer photos, UGC)
#### Payment Preferences
- Credit cards (Visa, Mastercard, Amex, Discover)
- PayPal (very common)
- Apple Pay, Google Pay (growing)
- Buy Now Pay Later: Klarna, Afterpay, Affirm
#### Content Style
- Direct, benefit-driven copy
- Bullet points for product features
- Short paragraphs
- Video content valued (product demos, reviews)
#### Mobile Behavior
- Mobile-first, high mobile commerce rate
- Thumb-friendly bottom navigation
- One-page checkout preferred

### Western Europe (UK, Germany, France, Netherlands, Scandinavia)
#### Color Preferences
- Sophisticated, muted palettes: navy, charcoal, forest green, burgundy
- Minimalist color schemes with strong typography
- Country-specific: France (elegant, refined), Germany (functional, clean), Scandinavia (hygge: warm minimalism, natural tones)
- Avoid: Overly aggressive red/orange for primary actions (except clearance/sale)
#### Layout Conventions
- Clean, structured, minimalist layouts
- Strong grid systems
- Less text-heavy than Asian markets
- Breadcrumb navigation expected
- Cookie consent banners mandatory (GDPR)
- VAT-inclusive pricing displayed
#### Typography
- Sans-serif: Roboto, Open Sans, Lato
- Smaller base text acceptable (14-15px)
- Strong typographic hierarchy
#### Trust Signals
- GDPR compliance notices (critical)
- Trusted Shops certification (Germany)
- Trustpilot, Feefo reviews
- Clear data privacy policy
- Local language support (critical — English not sufficient for DE/FR)
- Local payment methods
#### Payment Preferences
- Country-specific payment methods are CRITICAL:
  - Germany: SEPA, SOFORT, Giropay, PayPal
  - Netherlands: iDEAL (dominant), PayPal
  - France: Carte Bancaire, PayPal
  - UK: Credit cards, PayPal, BACS
  - Nordic: Vipps (Norway), MobilePay (Denmark), Swish (Sweden)
- Buy Now Pay Later: Klarna (originated in Sweden)
#### Content Style
- Formal, precise, detail-oriented (especially Germany)
- Product specifications valued highly
- Environmental/sustainability claims appreciated
- Less marketing hype, more factual
#### Regulatory Considerations
- GDPR compliance mandatory
- Cookie consent required
- VAT display rules
- Consumer protection laws (14-day return right in EU)
- Impressum (legal notice) required in Germany/Austria

### East Asia (Japan, South Korea, China)
#### Japan
##### Color Preferences
- Traditional: Red (#BC002D), White, Indigo
- Modern: Clean whites with accent colors
- Seasonal color awareness (cherry blossom pink in spring)
- Avoid: Pure white funeral associations in some contexts
##### Layout Conventions
- DENSE information layout (opposite of Western minimalism)
- Multiple sections per page, rich with details
- Small text acceptable, information-rich
- Cute (kawaii) elements for certain demographics
- Quality badges and certifications prominent
##### Trust Signals
- Japanese language mandatory (English alone will fail)
- Local phone number and address
- Product provenance and origin details
- Detailed specifications
- Customer reviews with photos
- SSL and local payment security badges
##### Payment
- Credit cards (JCB, Visa, Mastercard)
- Konbini (convenience store payment)
- Bank transfer (furikomi)
- PayPay, LINE Pay (growing mobile payments)
- Cash on delivery still used
##### Content
- Polite, formal language (keigo)
- Extremely detailed product descriptions
- Packaging and unboxing experience valued
- Seasonal and limited edition emphasis

#### South Korea
##### Color Preferences
- Vibrant, trend-following palettes
- K-beauty aesthetic: soft pastels, gradients
- Tech-savvy: dark mode acceptable for tech products
##### Layout Conventions
- Fast, dynamic, mobile-first
- Vertical scrolling preferred
- Rich media (video, live commerce)
- Naver-style layouts familiar to users
##### Trust Signals
- Korean language mandatory
- Naver reviews and blog mentions
- Local certifications
- Fast delivery promises (same-day common)
##### Payment
- Credit cards (dominant)
- KakaoPay, NaverPay (mobile wallets)
- Toss (bank transfer app)
- Mobile payment very high adoption
##### Content
- Trend-driven, fast-paced
- Live commerce and video content
- K-influencer endorsements

#### China (if accessible)
##### Color Preferences
- Red (#E60012) = luck, prosperity, celebration
- Gold = wealth, premium
- Avoid: Large amounts of white (funeral association in some contexts)
##### Layout Conventions
- EXTREMELY dense information layout
- Rich animations and micro-interactions
- Floating elements and pop-ups common
- QR codes everywhere
- Live streaming and video commerce
##### Trust Signals
- Chinese language mandatory (Simplified for mainland)
- ICP license display
- WeChat/Alipay payment badges
- Customer service via WeChat
- Taobao/Tmall-style review system
##### Payment
- Alipay, WeChat Pay (dominant mobile payments)
- UnionPay credit cards
- Cash on delivery (declining)
##### Content
- Livestreaming commerce critical
- Short video content (Douyin/TikTok)
- Group buy and flash sale mechanics
- Influencer (KOL) endorsements critical

### Southeast Asia (Indonesia, Thailand, Vietnam, Philippines, Malaysia)
#### Color Preferences
- Vibrant, warm colors
- Gold and jewel tones for premium feel
- Country-specific: Thailand (royal yellow, gold), Indonesia (red and white national colors)
#### Layout Conventions
- Mobile-first (mobile traffic dominant, 70%+)
- Simple navigation, large touch targets
- Fast-loading pages critical (many on 3G/4G)
- Cash-on-delivery prominent
#### Trust Signals
- Local language critical (Bahasa Indonesia, Thai, Vietnamese, Filipino, Malay)
- Cash on delivery availability prominently shown
- WhatsApp customer support (critical in Indonesia, Malaysia)
- Local social proof (Facebook/Instagram reviews)
- Free shipping promotions
#### Payment
- Cash on Delivery (COD) — VERY important, often 50%+ of orders
- Bank transfers
- E-wallets: GoPay/OVO (Indonesia), TrueMoney (Thailand), MoMo (Vietnam), GCash (Philippines)
- Credit card adoption growing but lower than Western markets
#### Content
- Visual-heavy, less text
- Social media integration (Facebook, Instagram, TikTok)
- Promotional and discount-focused
- Influencer marketing effective

### Middle East (UAE, Saudi Arabia, Egypt)
#### Color Preferences
- Gold, deep green, royal blue for premium
- White and green (Islamic associations)
- Rich, luxurious palettes for high-end
- Avoid: Pure black (mourning), excessive red
#### Layout Conventions
- RTL (Right-to-Left) layout for Arabic
- Bilingual (Arabic + English) common
- Luxury aesthetic for premium products
- Gender-sensitivity in imagery
#### Trust Signals
- Arabic language support (critical for local markets)
- COD availability (very common, 40%+ of ecommerce)
- Local phone numbers
- Halal certifications for relevant products
- Social media presence (Instagram, Snapchat)
#### Payment
- Cash on Delivery (dominant in Saudi Arabia, Egypt)
- Credit cards (growing in UAE)
- Mada (Saudi national payment system)
- Apple Pay, STC Pay (mobile wallets, growing)
#### Content
- Arabic content (Modern Standard Arabic or Gulf Arabic)
- English as secondary
- Religious and cultural sensitivity required
- Seasonal: Ramadan and Hajj promotions critical
- Luxury brand emphasis in GCC countries

### Latin America (Brazil, Mexico, Argentina, Colombia)
#### Color Preferences
- Warm, vibrant: orange, yellow, red, tropical greens
- Brazil: Green and yellow (national colors, but be careful with flag associations)
- Mexico: Vibrant colors, traditional patterns
#### Layout Conventions
- Mobile-first (high mobile usage)
- Installment payment display critical
- Simple, clear navigation
- Trust elements prominent
#### Trust Signals
- Local language (Portuguese for Brazil, Spanish for others)
- Customer reviews and ratings
- Secure payment badges
- Clear return policies
- Free shipping thresholds
- Local social proof (WhatsApp groups, Instagram)
#### Payment
- Installments (parcelas) — CRITICAL in Brazil and Mexico
- PIX (Brazil instant payment, dominant)
- Boleto bancário (Brazil)
- OXXO (Mexico cash payment)
- Credit cards (with installment options)
- Mercado Pago (regional wallet)
#### Content
- Portuguese (Brazil) or Spanish (Mexico, Argentina, Colombia)
- Emotional, relationship-driven copy
- Social media integration (WhatsApp, Instagram)
- Seasonal: Carnival (Brazil), Day of Dead (Mexico)
- Price sensitivity: discounts and promotions important

### Africa (Nigeria, South Africa, Kenya, Egypt)
#### Color Preferences
- Vibrant, bold colors
- South Africa: more Western-influenced, clean corporate
- Nigeria: bright, energetic colors
- Kenya: earth tones with vibrant accents
#### Layout Conventions
- Mobile-first (mobile-dominant, 60%+)
- Low-bandwidth optimization critical
- Simple, fast-loading pages
- Large touch targets
#### Trust Signals
- Local language or widely understood language (English in Nigeria/Kenya/South Africa)
- Cash on delivery availability
- Local phone numbers and WhatsApp support
- Social proof via social media
- Payment security badges
#### Payment
- Mobile money: M-Pesa (Kenya), mobile wallets (Nigeria)
- Cash on Delivery (common in Nigeria, Egypt)
- Bank transfers
- Credit cards (South Africa, growing elsewhere)
- Paystack, Flutterwave (Nigeria payment gateways)
#### Content
- English (Nigeria, Kenya, South Africa) or local languages
- WhatsApp business integration
- Social media commerce (Facebook, Instagram)
- Price sensitivity: promotions and discounts important

### Oceania (Australia, New Zealand)
#### Color Preferences
- Similar to North American preferences
- Earth tones and natural colors popular
- Beach/outdoor lifestyle imagery
#### Layout Conventions
- Clean, spacious layouts
- Mobile-optimized
- Clear pricing with GST included
#### Trust Signals
- Australian/NZ English
- Local shipping information
- Return policies
- Reviews (ProductReview.com.au)
- ACCC compliance for consumer guarantees
#### Payment
- Credit cards, PayPal
- Afterpay (Buy Now Pay Later, originated in Australia)
- Apple Pay, Google Pay
- Bank transfer (POLi)
#### Content
- Casual, friendly tone
- Outdoor/lifestyle imagery
- Sustainability claims valued

## Cross-Cultural Design Decision Framework

### Step 1: Identify Target Market
- Primary country/region
- Secondary markets
- Primary language(s)
- RTL vs LTR layout needed

### Step 2: Determine Design Direction
Based on target market, select:
- **Information density**: Dense (East Asia) vs Moderate (Europe) vs Spacious (North America, Oceania)
- **Color palette**: Match cultural associations and brand identity
- **Typography**: Latin scripts, CJK fonts, Arabic scripts
- **Imagery style**: Culturally appropriate, diverse representation
- **Trust elements**: Region-specific certifications and payment badges

### Step 3: Payment and Commerce
- Research dominant payment methods for target country
- COD availability (critical for SEA, Middle East, Africa, Latin America)
- Installment payments (critical for Latin America)
- Mobile wallet integration (critical for East Asia, Southeast Asia, Africa)
- Currency display conventions

### Step 4: Content and Communication
- Language: Professional translation, not auto-translate
- Tone: Formal (Germany, Japan) vs Casual (USA, Australia) vs Emotional (Latin America)
- Cultural references: Avoid culturally insensitive imagery or references
- Religious considerations: Halal (Middle East, SEA Muslim markets), Kosher (Israel)
- Seasonal awareness: Local holidays, festivals, shopping seasons

### Step 5: Legal and Regulatory
- GDPR (EU): Cookie consent, data privacy
- Impressum (Germany/Austria): Legal notice required
- CCPA (California): Data privacy
- PCI DSS: Payment security
- Local consumer protection laws
- VAT/Tax display requirements

### Step 6: Performance Considerations
- Image optimization for low-bandwidth markets (Africa, parts of SEA)
- CDN with local edge servers
- Mobile-first design for mobile-dominant markets
- Progressive loading for dense content markets

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid
- Using Western minimalism for East Asian markets (too sparse, lacks trust)
- Using dense layouts for Western markets (overwhelming, reduces trust)
- Forgetting RTL layout for Arabic markets
- Not offering COD in markets where it's dominant (SEA, Middle East, Africa, Latin America)
- Not offering installments in Brazil/Mexico (conversion killer)
- English-only content for non-English markets (Germany, France, Japan, Korea)
- Culturally insensitive color choices (e.g., excessive white in Chinese mourning contexts)
- Missing GDPR cookie consent for EU traffic
- Missing Impressum for German/Austrian sites
- Not optimizing for low-bandwidth in African markets
- Using generic stock photos that don't represent the target market's demographics

## Integration with WordPress Auto Site Builder

### During Intake
- Always ask for target market/country
- Ask for primary and secondary languages
- Ask about payment method requirements based on market
- Ask about COD availability and regions
- Ask about cultural sensitivity requirements

### During Design Preview
- Apply regional design preferences to the homepage style preview
- Show appropriate payment badges for the target market
- Use culturally appropriate imagery and copy
- Display correct currency and tax format
- Include required legal elements (GDPR, Impressum, etc.)

### During Build
- Configure WooCommerce for regional payment methods
- Set up appropriate shipping zones
- Configure language plugins if multilingual
- Implement RTL if targeting Arabic markets
- Optimize images for target market bandwidth
- Set up hreflang if multilingual
